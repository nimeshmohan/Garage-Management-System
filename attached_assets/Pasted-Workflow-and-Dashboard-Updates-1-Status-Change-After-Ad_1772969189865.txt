Workflow and Dashboard Updates
1. Status Change After Advisor Inspection

After the Advisor completes the inspection, the vehicle status should NOT immediately change to "Work in Progress."

Instead, the status should change to "Waiting for Job Allocation."

2. New Menu Tab

Add a new menu tab in the dashboard titled:

"Waiting for Job Allocation"

This tab should appear together with the existing tabs:

Pending Inspection

Waiting for Job Allocation

Work in Progress

Pending Final Inspection

Reopened

Delivered

History

3. History Section

In the History tab, display all vehicles from the current month only.

Technician Dashboard Changes
4. Rename Waiting for Parts

In the Technician Dashboard, rename the status/button:

Waiting for Parts → Job Stop

5. Job Stop Function

When the Job Stop button is clicked:

A dialog box should appear

The dialog must contain a field:

Reason for Stop

The technician must enter the reason for stopping the job.

6. Stop Time Tracking

Stop time does not need to be tracked.

Only the reason for job stoppage needs to be recorded.

Job Controller Workflow
7. Job Stop Notification to Job Controller

When a Technician stops a job:

The vehicle/job card should immediately appear in the Job Controller Dashboard.

The status must update across all dashboards (Advisor, Technician, Reception, Job Controller).

8. Job Stoppage Menu

In the Job Controller Dashboard, create a menu tab called:

"Job Stoppage"

All job-stopped vehicles/cards should appear in this section.

9. Reassign Technician

The Job Controller should be able to:

Review the stopped job

Reassign the job to another technician if required

Job History Tracking in Job Controller

Each job card in the Job Controller Dashboard should include a Job History section showing:

Previous jobs completed

Which technician performed the job

Time taken to complete the job

If a job stop occurred

Reason for the job stoppage

This history helps the Job Controller understand past actions and manage job reallocation efficiently.
